# Insights and Recommendations

## 1. Activation is the first major product hurdle

Some signed-up users never submit their first question, which means the product does not reach its first value moment for those users.

Recommendation: Improve onboarding with clearer question examples, subject selection, and a first-doubt prompt immediately after signup.

## 2. Wait time directly affects completion

Sessions with longer tutor wait times have lower completion rates and weaker ratings.

Recommendation: Keep wait time below 120 seconds by improving tutor availability forecasting and showing realistic wait-time expectations.

## 3. Math and Physics create the highest demand

Math and Physics questions appear more frequently than other subjects, creating higher risk of tutor supply shortages.

Recommendation: Prioritize tutor hiring, scheduling, and incentives for high-demand subjects.

## 4. First-session experience affects retention

Users who complete a session successfully are more likely to return than users who abandon before connection or drop mid-session.

Recommendation: Treat first-session completion as a core product KPI and design interventions around it.

## 5. Payment timing matters

Payment conversion is stronger when users first experience a successful learning outcome.

Recommendation: Show paid plan offers after a completed session or high feedback rating rather than before value is delivered.

## 6. Acquisition quality varies by channel

Some acquisition channels generate high signup volume but weaker activation or payment conversion.

Recommendation: Evaluate channels on activated users, completed sessions, and revenue instead of signup volume alone.

## 7. Churn segments are actionable

Users who ask one question but never complete a session are a clear re-engagement target.

Recommendation: Build lifecycle campaigns for users stuck after question submission, tutor wait, or first incomplete session.
