# Product Case Study

## Context

The product is a synthetic EdTech doubt-solving app where students submit academic questions and connect with tutors for live help.

## Problem

Users may install the app or create an account but fail to reach the first successful learning session. Drop-offs can happen during onboarding, question submission, tutor wait time, session quality, repeat usage, or payment.

## Objective

Identify the biggest drop-off points and recommend product improvements that increase activation, retention, and paid conversion.

## User Journey

1. Student installs the app.
2. Student signs up.
3. Student completes onboarding.
4. Student submits a doubt.
5. App searches for an available tutor.
6. Student connects with a tutor.
7. Student completes a session.
8. Student returns for another session.
9. Student purchases a paid plan.

## Metrics

- Activation Rate
- Tutor Connection Rate
- Session Completion Rate
- Repeat Usage Rate
- Payment Conversion Rate
- Average Wait Time
- Average Session Rating
- Revenue
- ARPPU
- Churn Rate

## Analysis

The SQL analysis covers acquisition mix, signup-to-question activation, full funnel conversion, subject-level demand, wait-time impact, weekly retention cohorts, churn segments, grade-wise usage, and payment conversion.

## Insights

- The first question is a key activation milestone.
- Wait time has a strong relationship with abandonment.
- Math and Physics show higher demand and require stronger tutor supply planning.
- Completed first sessions improve the chance of repeat usage.
- Payment conversion is stronger when users experience successful value before seeing paid offers.

## Recommendations

- Reduce tutor wait time, especially for Math and Physics.
- Improve onboarding for acquisition channels with low activation.
- Send re-engagement nudges to users who asked a question but did not complete a session.
- Align tutor staffing with subject demand and peak usage periods.
- Trigger payment offers after successful first-session completion.

## Expected Product Impact

These actions should improve activation, increase completed sessions, strengthen retention, reduce churn, and improve paid conversion by presenting monetization at a higher-intent moment.
